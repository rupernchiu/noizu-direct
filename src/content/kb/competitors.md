## The choice a creator faces

When a Malaysian cosplayer wants to sell a 200-print run of convention photos, their realistic options are:

1. **DM sales** (Twitter / Telegram / IG) — current default.
2. **Booth** — only viable if they read Japanese well and don't mind paying outbound FX.
3. **Gumroad** — workable for digital, awful for physical or for receiving payout into a MY bank.
4. **Etsy** — creator-unfriendly fees + listing economics.
5. **Shopee / Carousell** — wrong audience, wrong margin.
6. **Build their own with Shopify/Lemonsqueezy** — works for the top 5%, overhead-heavy for everyone else.
7. **noizu.direct** — what we're building.

## Our positioning

We're not competing on **catalog scale** (Shopee wins by orders of magnitude) or on **global brand** (Etsy wins). We compete on **creator economics + SEA-native flows + niche fit**.

The pitch to a creator:
> "You already sell to your audience over DM. We give you the storefront, the rail, and the dispute backstop — without making you build it yourself or move to a Western platform that doesn't speak ringgit."

The pitch to a buyer:
> "Same creators you already follow. Pay with the wallet you already use. Get protection if it doesn't arrive."

## Defensibility

- **Local-rail integration depth.** Once a creator's payouts route through our Airwallex flow, switching costs are real.
- **Reputation/follow graph.** A creator who built 500 followers on noizu.direct loses them by leaving.
- **SEA-tuned operations.** Every rail/tax/SWIFT decision is local-aware — a generic global competitor won't replicate cheaply.
- **Niche brand.** Being *the* SEA cosplay/doujin marketplace is more defensible than being one of fifty general indie marketplaces.

## What would change the landscape

- **Booth aggressively localizes outside Japan** — biggest realistic threat.
- **Gumroad fixes APAC payout** — meaningful but not fatal; we'd still win on physical goods.
- **A regional player (Shopee, Tokopedia) launches a "creator marketplace" sub-brand** — fight on brand and creator economics.
- **Stripe meaningfully expands local-rail support in MY/PH** — would make build-your-own easier; mitigated by our integrated UX.
